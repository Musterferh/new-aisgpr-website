Who We Are

Our Story

Climate Warden began in Bauchi State as a grassroots response to the visible environmental challenges facing Bauchi State, Nigeria. Recognizing that the youth are the most significant stakeholders in our climate future, we launched this initiative to bridge the gap between climate awareness and community action.

What started as a local movement has evolved into a structured organization dedicated to fostering a generation of environmentally conscious leaders — empowered to build a resilient and sustainable Northern Nigeria.

How We Work

Our Approach

A proven three-step model turning classroom awareness into measurable community action.

01

Educate

We bring structured climate education directly into primary and secondary schools through engaging workshops, curriculum integration, and teacher capacity-building sessions.

Schools · Classrooms · Curriculum
02

Mobilise

Trained students form Sustainability Clubs and receive leadership development, turning knowledge into organised, youth-driven environmental advocacy within their schools.

Clubs · Leadership · Training
03

Impact

Clubs lead real-world action — tree planting drives, community clean-up campaigns, and local policy advocacy — creating lasting environmental change across Bauchi State.
